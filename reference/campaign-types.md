# Campaign Types

Google Ads offers several campaign types, each designed for a different advertising channel and goal. This page explains each type, when to use it, and what you need to get started.

---

## Quick Comparison

| Type | Channel | Best For | Key Requirements |
|------|---------|----------|------------------|
| **Search** | Google Search results | High-intent users actively searching | Keywords + text ads |
| **Display** | Websites and apps | Brand awareness, remarketing | Images + logos |
| **Video** | YouTube and partner sites | Storytelling, engagement | Video creative |
| **Shopping** | Google Shopping tab, Search | E-commerce product listings | Product feed |
| **Performance Max** | All Google channels | Maximizing conversions, automation | Images + logos + headlines |

---

## Search Campaigns

Search campaigns show **text ads** in Google Search results when someone searches for keywords you are targeting. They are the most common and straightforward campaign type.

![Search campaign example](../images/ref-campaign-type-search.png)
<!-- TODO: Add screenshot of a search ad appearing in Google Search results -->

### How It Works

1. You define **keywords** that describe your product or service
2. When someone searches for those keywords (or related terms), your text ad may appear
3. You only pay when someone clicks your ad (cost-per-click)

### Networks

- **Google Search** -- Your ads appear in Google Search results
- **Search Partners** -- Optionally extend to Google's search partner sites

### Requirements

- At least one **ad group** with keywords
- At least one **responsive search ad** per ad group (headlines + descriptions)
- A **landing page URL** for each ad

### Best For

- Businesses targeting people who are **actively searching** for their product or service
- Lead generation and direct sales
- Local services ("plumber near me", "dentist in Berlin")
- Any scenario where **user intent is high**

### Tips

- Start with 10-20 well-researched keywords per ad group
- Use a mix of [match types](keyword-match-types.md) for balanced reach and control
- Write at least 3 headlines and 2 descriptions per ad for Google's optimization

## Display Campaigns

Display campaigns show **visual banner ads** across millions of websites, apps, and Google-owned properties in the Google Display Network (GDN).

![Display campaign example](../images/ref-campaign-type-display.png)
<!-- TODO: Add screenshot of a display banner ad on a website -->

### How It Works

1. You create **image ads** or responsive display ads with images, logos, and text
2. Google places your ads on relevant websites and apps across its network
3. You can target by audience interests, demographics, topics, or specific websites

### Networks

- **Google Display Network** -- Over 2 million websites and apps

### Requirements

- **Marketing images** in landscape format (1.91:1 aspect ratio recommended)
- **Logo** in square format (1:1 aspect ratio)
- Headlines and descriptions for responsive display ads
- A landing page URL

### Best For

- **Brand awareness** -- Getting your name in front of a large audience
- **Remarketing** -- Showing ads to people who have already visited your website
- Visual products or services that benefit from imagery
- Reaching users who are **browsing** but not actively searching

### Tips

- Use high-quality, eye-catching images
- Keep text on images minimal -- Google may reject ads with too much text
- Set up remarketing audiences for best return on investment

## Video Campaigns

Video campaigns show **video ads** on YouTube and Google's video partner sites. They are ideal for storytelling, product demonstrations, and building brand recognition.

![Video campaign example](../images/ref-campaign-type-video.png)
<!-- TODO: Add screenshot of a video ad playing on YouTube -->

### How It Works

1. You upload a **video** to YouTube
2. You configure targeting (demographics, interests, topics, keywords)
3. Your video ad plays before, during, or alongside YouTube content

### Ad Formats

| Format | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Skippable in-stream** | Plays before/during videos. Viewer can skip after 5 seconds. |
| **Non-skippable in-stream** | 15-second ads that must be watched in full. |
| **Bumper ads** | 6-second non-skippable ads. |
| **In-feed video** | Appears in YouTube search results and recommendations. |

### Requirements

- A **YouTube video** (uploaded to your YouTube channel)
- Targeting settings (audiences, demographics, topics)
- A landing page URL (optional, for driving traffic)

### Best For

- **Storytelling** -- Explaining complex products or services
- **Brand building** -- Creating emotional connections with your audience
- **Product demos** -- Showing your product in action
- Reaching a **massive audience** -- YouTube has over 2 billion monthly users

### Tips

- Capture attention in the first 5 seconds (before the skip button appears)
- Include a clear call-to-action and keep videos to 15-30 seconds for best performance

## Shopping Campaigns

Shopping campaigns show **product listing ads** with images, prices, and your store name. They appear in the Google Shopping tab, Google Search results, and partner sites.

![Shopping campaign example](../images/ref-campaign-type-shopping.png)
<!-- TODO: Add screenshot of a shopping product listing ad in Google Search results -->

### How It Works

1. You set up a **product feed** in Google Merchant Center with your product data
2. Google automatically matches your products to relevant searches
3. Your product appears with image, price, and store name -- no keywords needed

### Requirements

- A **Google Merchant Center** account with an approved product feed
- Product data: title, description, price, image, availability, link
- A connected Google Ads account

### Best For

- **E-commerce businesses** selling physical products
- Products where **price and visually appearance** drive purchase decisions
- Competing directly with other retailers in product comparisons

### Tips

- Use high-quality product images with clean white backgrounds
- Optimize product titles and descriptions for search relevance
- Keep pricing and availability up to date in your feed

> **Note:** Shopping campaigns require a Google Merchant Center account, which is configured outside of Defo Ads. Defo Ads can help you plan and manage the campaign structure.

## Performance Max (PMax) Campaigns

Performance Max is Google's **AI-driven campaign type** that automatically runs your ads across all Google channels -- Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps, and Discover -- from a single campaign.

![Performance Max campaign example](../images/ref-campaign-type-pmax.png)

### How It Works

1. You provide **assets** -- images, logos, headlines, descriptions, and videos
2. Google's AI automatically creates ad combinations from your assets
3. The AI optimizes placement, targeting, and bidding across all channels
4. You set a goal (conversions, leads, sales) and Google handles the rest

### Networks

- **All Google channels** -- Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Google Maps, Discover

### Requirements

- **Images** -- Landscape and square formats. Google recommends **15 or more images** for best performance.
- **Logos** -- Square format (1:1)
- **Headlines** -- Up to 15 headlines (max 30 characters each)
- **Long headlines** -- Up to 5 long headlines (max 90 characters each)
- **Descriptions** -- Up to 5 descriptions (max 90 characters each)
- A **landing page URL**
- A **conversion goal** configured in Google Ads

### Best For

- Businesses that want **maximum reach** across all Google properties
- Advertisers comfortable with **AI-driven optimization** and less manual control
- E-commerce with existing conversion tracking
- Campaigns where you want Google to find the **best-performing combinations**

### Tips

- Provide as many assets as possible -- more assets give the AI more to work with
- 15+ images is the recommended minimum for strong performance
- Set clear conversion goals so the AI knows what to optimize for
- Monitor performance and add new assets regularly to keep results fresh

## Which Campaign Type Should You Choose?

Use this decision guide to pick the right campaign type for your goals:

### Start Here

1. **Are you selling physical products online?**
   - Yes, and you have a product feed --> **Shopping**
   - Yes, but no product feed --> **Search** or **Performance Max**

2. **Do you want to appear when people search for your service?**
   - Yes --> **Search**

3. **Do you want to build brand awareness with visuals?**
   - Yes, with images --> **Display**
   - Yes, with video --> **Video**

4. **Do you want Google's AI to handle everything?**
   - Yes, and you can provide lots of assets --> **Performance Max**

5. **Not sure where to start?**
   - Start with **Search**. It is the most direct, measurable, and easiest to understand. You can always add other campaign types later.

Most successful advertisers use **multiple campaign types** together. For example, combine Display (awareness) with Search (conversion), or use Performance Max to cover all channels from a single campaign.
